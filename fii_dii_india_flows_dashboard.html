🌐 Sector Flow

🌐 Sector-Wise FII / FPI Allocation

Source: NSDL Fortnightly FPI Reports · BSE 22-Sector Classification · via Screener.in (C-MOTS) · Updated twice a month
How is this data calculated?  SEBI mandates all Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) to hold securities via Indian depositories (NSDL / CDSL). Every fortnight, depositories publish sector-wise Assets Under Custody (AUC). The % of AUM shown is each sector's share of the total FPI equity portfolio in India. Sectors are classified using BSE's Common Industry Classification (~22 sectors, ~4,800 issuers; unlisted / others go to the "Others" bucket). Flows show net FPI inflows/outflows per sector over the last 15-day reporting window and the rolling 12-month period.

1
Live Sync Engine & Status Pill

On every page load, the terminal fires an authenticated request to nseindia.com/api/fiidiiTradeReact via the allorigins.win CORS proxy.

LIVE SYNC — Today's session data was fetched successfully. Numbers update automatically.
LOCAL ARCHIVE — Markets are closed or NSE returned no new data. Dashboard uses the embedded 15-day history.
💡 Pro Tip: Hit Force Sync in the top-right to manually trigger a fresh data pull mid-session.
2
Flow Strength Meter

The animated dual-bar gauge in the Hero card plots the relative aggression of FII vs DII in absolute flow terms. It answers: "Who is driving the market today?"

FII BAR (RED)
Shows FII's share of total combined flow. A 70%+ reading = FIIs are the dominant force (selling or buying).
DII BAR (GREEN)
A wide DII bar means domestic funds are responding heavily — often absorbing FII sell-offs or co-buying in rallies.
💡 Reading Signal: FII Selling 80% + DII Buying 20% → Panic Sell with little DII support. FII Selling 80% + DII Buying 80% → Structural Rotation, not a free fall.
3
FII Streak Counter

Tracks the number of consecutive trading sessions FII has remained on one side (All Buy or All Sell). Streaks are powerful market signals.

🔴 SELL STREAK: The longer the sell streak and higher the velocity, the more likely an oversold bounce. A 10+ day streak at ₹5,000+ Cr/day = historically extreme.
🟢 BUY STREAK: Sustained buying with high velocity is a strong confirmation of trend reversal or foreign re-entry into India.
The Streak Velocity shows the cumulative net flow (in ₹ Cr) during the streak — measuring the total damage or firepower.
4
Structural Insight Cards

These four cards provide a quick snapshot of key market dynamics: Regime Signal (overall market health), FII/DII Divergence (how much they disagree), FII Sell Pressure (intensity of foreign selling), and DII Absorption (domestic buying strength).

💡 Pro Tip: Look for extreme readings in "FII Sell Pressure" combined with high "DII Absorption" for potential turning points.
5
F&O Derivatives Positioning

This section provides a granular view of FII and DII positioning in the Futures & Options market. It's broken down into 8 sub-cards covering Index Futures, Stock Futures, Index Options, and Stock Options for both FII and DII.

FII F&O
Tracks FII's net long/short positions in Index Futures, Stock Futures, Index Calls/Puts, and Stock Calls/Puts. Crucial for understanding their directional bias and hedging activity.
DII F&O
Monitors DII's derivatives activity. While DIIs are primarily cash market players, their F&O positions can offer insights into their short-term views or hedging strategies.
💡 Reading Signal: A significant build-up in FII Index Futures shorts often precedes market corrections, especially if accompanied by heavy selling in the cash market.

7
Smart Data Filters & Matrix

The "Databases & Matrices" tab holds the raw numbers in Daily, Weekly, Monthly, and Annual views.

All Data Shows every trading session in the archive. Full chronological data.
FII Bloodbath Only sessions where FII net sold more than ₹5,000 Cr — the true capitulation days.
DII Absorption Days where DIIs bought more than ₹5,000 Cr — showing strong domestic conviction.
Extreme Divergence FII sold ≥ ₹8,000 Cr AND DII bought ≥ ₹8,000 Cr — pure structural rotation days, rare and very significant.
8
Historical Charts

The "Historical Charts" tab provides visual trends of FII and DII activity over time. The Monthly Net Flows chart shows the net buying/selling activity each month, while the Multi-Year Cumulative Divergence chart illustrates the long-term trend of FII extraction versus DII injection, highlighting structural shifts in market participation.

💡 Pro Tip: Observe the cumulative divergence chart for sustained trends. A widening gap where FIIs are net sellers and DIIs are net buyers indicates strong domestic support.
